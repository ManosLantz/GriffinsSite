Rugby Rules

Rugby comes in two main forms — Union and League. They share the same DNA but play very differently. Here are the basics of each.

15-a-side

Rugby Union

Rugby Union is played by two teams of 15 players. The aim is to carry, pass or kick an oval ball over the opponent's goal line and touch it down for a try. The ball can only be passed backwards or sideways — never forwards — so teams advance through running, support play and territory-winning kicks.

How play continues

When a player is tackled, possession is contested on the ground at the breakdown, forming a ruck (players over the ball) or a maul (a tackled player held up while team-mates drive forward). There is no limit on how long a team can keep the ball, as long as they win each contest.

Set pieces

  • Scrum — 8 forwards from each side bind and push to restart play after a minor infringement. Scrums are fully contested.
  • Line-out — the ball is thrown back into play between two lines of jumping forwards after it goes out of bounds (into touch).

Scoring

ScorePoints
Try5
Conversion2
Penalty kick3
Drop goal3

Good to know

  • Governed worldwide by World Rugby.
  • Has a fast seven-a-side variant, Rugby Sevens, which is an Olympic sport.
13-a-side

Rugby League

Rugby League is played by two teams of 13 players. The objective is the same — ground the ball over the opponent's line for a try — but the rules are designed for a faster, more open and continuous game with fewer stoppages.

How play continues

Instead of contesting the ball on the ground, a tackled player stands up and rolls the ball back with their foot — the play-the-ball. Crucially, each team gets a set of six tackles to make progress; if they haven't scored by the sixth, possession turns over to the other side. This drives teams to attack with urgency.

Set pieces

  • Scrum — used far less often and is uncontested, simply restarting play rather than being a battle for the ball.
  • No line-outs — play is restarted differently when the ball goes out of bounds.

Scoring

ScorePoints
Try4
Conversion2
Penalty goal2
Drop / field goal1

Good to know

  • Governed worldwide by the International Rugby League.
  • Generally quicker and more structured, with less contest for possession.
